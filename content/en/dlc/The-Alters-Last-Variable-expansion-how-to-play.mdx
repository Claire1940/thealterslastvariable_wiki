<Section icon={Activity} title="Cycle 1: The 14-Day Survival Deadline">

The most critical aspect of **The Alters Last Variable expansion how to play** is managing the opening 14 days. You are tasked with the "Forced Dormancy" objective, which requires the construction of a Cryosleep Chamber. Failure to complete this before the planetary hot phase begins results in a game over. Your initial focus must be split between immediate repairs and establishing a sustainable Metal and Rapidium income.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Repair the Research Core">
    Locate the damaged Research Lab and use 10 Metals to craft a Repair Kit. This unlocks the ability to research the **New Branching Procedure**, which is essential for creating your first specialist Alter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish Resource Outposts">
    Travel south of the base to find Metal and Rapidium deposits. Build automated outposts immediately. Manual gathering is too slow to meet the 14-day construction requirements for the Cryosleep Chamber.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Create your First Alter">
    Use the Quantum Computer to create an Alter. For Cycle 1, the **Jan Geologist** is the optimal choice to boost Metal mining efficiency by 50%, allowing for faster base expansion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Oasis Investigation">
    Enter the Oasis biome to collect three Electric Algae samples. Use these at the Lab to research **Phased Rapidium**, a material required for both the Cryosleep Chamber and the first Terraformer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Resource Management">
Never let your Metal reserve drop below 10. You always need enough for an emergency Repair Kit in case an earthquake damages your power supply or life support modules.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Microscope} title="Specialist Alters and Work Assignments">

Unlike the base game, these Alters are often assigned to **Field Labs**—remote research stations where they work independently for entire cycles.

<Section icon={Map} title="Terraforming Arenas and Resource Routes">

The map consists of nine distinct arenas. Except for the Oasis, every region must be terraformed using one of three Terraformer tiers. Each successful transformation changes the landscape, opening new paths to rare resources and Field Lab sites.

| Arena | Terraformer Tier | Key Resources | Field Lab Access |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Oasis** | None (Reference) | Electric Algae | No |
| **Rocky Valley** | Tier I | Metals, Obsidian | Yes (Lab 1) |
| **Base Arena** | Tier I | Organics, Debris | No |
| **Misty Gorge** | Tier II | Enriched Metal, Polymer Gas | Yes (Lab 2) |
| **Volcano** | Tier II | Minerals, Obsidian | No |
| **Far Hill** | Tier III | Mushrooms, Megafluid | Yes (Lab 3) |

To progress the "how to play" loop, you must balance the energy cost of Terraformers with the base's power capacity. Tier III Terraformers require **Megafluid**, which can only be produced after completing research in at least two Field Labs.

<Callout type="info" title="Environmental Hazards">
Terraforming triggers planetary reactions. Expect increased radiation waves and earthquakes immediately following a successful transformation. Ensure your **Radiation Filters** are stocked before activating a new Terraformer.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Endings and Achievement Guide">

The expansion concludes with a final confrontation regarding the "Last Variable"—the ultimate nature of the Oasis. There are two primary endings, determined by your final interaction with JanBot after activating the **ENC Generator**.

**Ending Paths:**
1.  **Create a Successor**: Jan chooses to perpetuate the cycle of scientific obsession, ensuring the research continues beyond his own life through a new Alter.
2.  **Break the Cycle**: Jan accepts the limits of human knowledge and refuses to create another life to carry the burden of the Oasis, effectively ending the experiment.
