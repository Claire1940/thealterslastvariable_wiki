<Section icon={Map} title="The Alters Last Variable Scientist Story Premise">

The narrative of this paid expansion shifts focus away from the collective escape of the base game, centering instead on the solitary path of Jan Scientist. Having made the fateful decision to remain on the hostile planet, Jan must utilize the underground research base and advanced cloning technology to unlock the secrets of the Oasis—a unique, resilient reference biome at the heart of the planet's ecosystem.

This campaign spans approximately 20 hours of tactical survival, base expansion, and psychological tension. As Jan Scientist, you do not just fight the elements; you wrestle with the ethical boundaries of creation, scientific obsession, and the physical toll of long-term survival.

<Section icon={Target} title="Cycle-by-Cycle Narrative Walkthrough">

The story of Jan Scientist progresses through structured planetary cycles. Each cycle requires balancing main story quests with strict resource deadlines to prepare for the inevitable cryosleep phase.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Cycle 1: The First Branch">
    Following the death of the Old Scientist, Jan Scientist bakes a plan to repair the ruined Research Lab. After establishing a basic Metals Outpost, you research the **New Branching Procedure** to clone your first Alter. You must gather three biological samples from the Oasis, analyze them, and build both the Sample Analysis Module and the **Cryosleep Chamber** within a strict 14-day deadline.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Cycle 2: Oasis 2.0 & Field Labs">
    Upon waking, you discover root barriers blocking deeper Oasis paths. After researching root-drilling techniques, you construct the first **Field Laboratory** in Rocky Valley. Assigning a specialist Alter to this external lab allows them to gather long-term data, though they will age while Jan Scientist rests in cryosleep.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Cycle 3: The Proxilium Breakthrough">
    With the discovery of Proxilium, base engineering expands. You must construct a Proxilium Tank and a Splitter to process advanced materials. Upgrading your Sample Analysis Module with a Magnetometer allows you to complete a deep 12-hour Magnetometric Analysis, pushing Jan closer to understanding the planetary core.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Advanced Cycles: Megafluid & High-Tier Terraforming">
    As the planet's defensive reactions intensify, you deploy Tier III Terraformers in Far Hill and Waterfall. Collecting data from multiple Field Labs unlocks **Megafluid** production. You must balance high-radiation exploration sweeps with base maintenance to assemble the massive ENC Generator.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Main Quest | Key Objective | Resource / Time Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Famous Last Words** | Bury Old Scientist, inspect lab | 10 Metals (Repair Kit) |
| **Forced Dormancy** | Build Cryosleep Chamber | 50 Metals, 65 Rapidium |
| **Root of the Problem** | Research root-barrier drilling | 2 Working Hours |
| **Colossal Energy** | Install Tier III Terraformers | 90 Metals, 200 Rapidium, 75 Megafluid |

<Callout type="tip" title="Resource Management Tip">
Never spend your starting Metals on optional base decorations or early comfort modules. Keep a reserve of at least 10 Metals on Day 1 to ensure you can craft the Repair Kit needed to unlock the Research Lab.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="Field Lab Assignments">
When you assign an Alter to a Field Laboratory, they leave the base permanently for that cycle. Ensure you complete their personal conversations and outstanding workshop tasks before sending them out, as they will age and remain inaccessible for daily base chores.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Underground Base and Cryosleep Preparation">

The underground base serves as your shield against the planet's extreme climate. Survival requires strict adherence to a logistics checklist before triggering the end of each exploration phase.

| Preparation Category | Requirement | Failure Consequence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cryosleep Organics** | 50 Organics per person in base | Inability to enter cryosleep / Game Over |
| **Base Maintenance** | Structural repairs & power balancing | Mid-cycle blackouts and module damage |
| **Surface Cleanup** | Dismantle threatened surface extractors | Permanent loss of construction materials |
| **Crew Readiness** | Feed, heal, and resolve Alter conflicts | Alter Revolt and halted production |

<Callout type="warning" title="The Golden Rule of Organics">
Treat your Organics reserve as completely untouchable during the final three days of a cycle. Spending your Organics on optional Greenhouse experiments or high-tier food recipes can leave you short of the threshold required to put your crew into cryosleep.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Endings, Final Choices, and Achievements">

The climax of **The Alters Last Variable scientist story** occurs after assembling and activating the ENC Generator in Rocky Valley. Jan Scientist descends into the deepest sector of the planet to make a final, defining decision regarding the Oasis project.

### The Two Concluding Paths

*   **Ending 1: Create a Successor**
    Jan accepts the model of scientific succession. By cloning a new Alter and leaving them under the guidance of JanBot, Jan ensures that the research program survives his own lifetime. Obsession wins over mortality, viewing the pursuit of knowledge as an infinite chain of created lives.
*   **Ending 2: Break the Cycle**
    Jan rejects the endless loop of cloning and research. Recognizing the ethical cost of creating lives simply to serve as tools for a hostile planet, Jan shuts down the succession protocol. He accepts human mortality and leaves the Oasis's ultimate questions unanswered.
