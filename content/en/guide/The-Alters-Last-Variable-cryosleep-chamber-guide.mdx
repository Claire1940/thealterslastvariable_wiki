<Section icon={Target} title="The Critical 14-Day Deadline">

In *The Alters: Last Variable*, the Cryosleep Chamber is not just another base module; it is the fundamental requirement for Jan Scientist's survival. The expansion introduces a harsh planetary cycle where 13-year-long "hot phases" force the crew into suspended animation. Your first major objective, the **Forced Dormancy** quest, gives you exactly 14 days to establish this facility. Failing to meet this deadline results in an immediate game over, as the base cannot shield the crew from the surface's lethal temperatures without the chamber.

| Requirement Type | Value/Detail | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quest Name** | Forced Dormancy | Main Story Milestone |
| **Time Limit** | 14 Days (Cycle 1) | Hard Deadline |
| **Primary Goal** | Build Cryosleep Chamber | Survival Essential |
| **Secondary Goal** | Stock Organics Reserve | Crew Safety |
| **Failure State** | Death by Radiation/Heat | Critical Risk |

Managing your time during these first two weeks is vital. You must balance the immediate need for **The Alters Last Variable cryosleep chamber guide** logistics with the expansion of your resource outposts. If you spend too much Metal on optional comfort modules or early base decorations, you may find yourself short of the materials needed for the chamber as the deadline looms.

<Callout type="warning" title="Deadline Alert">
Do not treat the 14-day timer as a suggestion. The planetary cooldown ends abruptly, and if the chamber is not fully operational and powered, you will lose your entire progress.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Construction Costs and Soil Logistics">

Building the Cryosleep Chamber requires careful planning of your underground base layout. Unlike surface structures, underground modules are restricted by soil types. You must ensure you have a valid placement cell connected to your main power and corridor network. The chamber has a significant footprint, so blocking off potential expansion routes with poor placement in the early game can lead to expensive rebuilding later.

| Material | Amount Required | Primary Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Metals** | 50 | Metals Outpost (South of Base) |
| **Rapidium** | 65 | Rapidium Deposits (Oasis/South) |
| **Power** | Constant | Base Power Grid |
| **Soil Type** | Stable/Standard | Underground Excavation |

<Callout type="tip" title="Physicist Bonus">

<Section icon={Activity} title="Organics Management and Crew Scaling">

The most common point of failure for new players isn't building the chamber, but failing to fuel it. Each person entering the chamber requires a specific amount of **Organics** to sustain their biological functions during the 13-year sleep. As you create more specialist Alters, your Organics requirement increases linearly.

| Crew Composition | Organics Required | Survival Buffer (Recommended) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Jan Scientist Only** | 50 | 75 |
| **Jan + 1 Alter** | 100 | 130 |
| **Jan + 2 Alters** | 150 | 190 |
| **Jan + 3 Alters** | 200 | 250 |
| **Jan + 4 Alters** | 250 | 320 |

  <Step num="2" title="Calculate Crew Needs">

If you find yourself short on Organics on the final day, you must make a difficult choice: dismantle surface structures to recover resources for a last-minute push, or risk an **Alter Revolt** if a crew member realizes there isn't enough fuel to keep them alive.

<Section icon={Zap} title="Field Labs and External Assignments">

A unique mechanic in *The Alters: Last Variable* is the **Field Laboratory**. These structures are placed in terraformed regions like Rocky Valley or Misty Gorge. When an Alter is assigned to a Field Lab, they remain on the surface for the duration of the hot phase. This has significant strategic implications for your cryosleep planning.

**Strategic Advantages of Field Labs:**
- **Zero Organics Cost**: An Alter in a Field Lab does not use the Cryosleep Chamber and therefore does not consume the 50 Organics fee.
- **Continuous Research**: The assigned specialist continues to work on long-term Oasis projects while Jan Scientist sleeps.
- **Resource Savings**: By keeping specialists outside, you can maintain a larger crew with a smaller Organics stockpile.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Jan Geologist">
    Best for the **Rocky Valley** Field Lab. He accelerates Phased Rapidium research and clears terrain barriers for the next cycle.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Jan Chemist">
    Ideal for **Misty Gorge**. He reduces Field Lab research time from 10 hours to approximately 6 hours and 15 minutes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Jan Biologist">
    Essential for late-game **Waterfall** research. He analyzes biological samples that unlock the final terraforming tiers.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

However, be warned: an Alter stationed in a Field Lab will **visibly age** and cannot participate in base maintenance or relationship events until the next cool phase begins. Ensure their personal storylines are advanced as much as possible before sending them out.

<Callout type="info" title="Expert Strategy">
Use Field Labs to "park" specialists you cannot afford to feed in the Cryosleep Chamber. This allows you to keep all four specialist types active without needing 250 Organics every cycle.
</Callout>
