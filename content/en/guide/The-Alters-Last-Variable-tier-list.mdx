<Section icon={Target} title="Creation Priority and Work Efficiency">

Choosing the order of your Alters is the most critical decision in Cycle 1. If you pick a specialist whose bonus you cannot utilize (e.g., a Biologist when you have no Greenhouse), you waste precious Rapidium and working hours. 

The Geologist should always be your first choice because the early game is entirely gated by Metal. Without Metal, you cannot build the Repair Kits needed to fix the Research Lab, nor can you construct the pylons required to reach the first Rapidium deposit. The Physicist follows closely because as the base expands, maintenance becomes a constant drain on Jan’s time.

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Warning">
Creating an Alter costs significant Rapidium. Do not branch your first Alter until you have secured the Metals Outpost south of the base, or you will run out of materials to build the necessary life-support modules for your new crew member.
</Callout>

<Callout type="success" title="Exploration Tip">
Always explore a newly terraformed region immediately during the "cool phase." Once the hot phase begins and you enter cryosleep, the planetary defense reactions (earthquakes and radiation) make exploration significantly more dangerous.
</Callout>

### Research Priorities for the Oasis Project

Scientific progression is non-linear but follows a specific dependency chain. Focus your Research Lab on these projects in order:
1. **New Branching Procedure**: Unlocks the ability to create specialists.
2. **Analysis of Samples**: Required to understand the Oasis ecosystem.
3. **Phased Rapidium**: Unlocks Tier I Terraformers.
4. **Field Laboratory**: Essential for stationing Alters and advancing the story.
5. **Proxylium / Megafluid**: Late-game materials for the ENC Generator.

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Underground Base and Cryosleep Logistics">

The underground base is your only sanctuary, but it requires constant upkeep. Unlike the mobile base in the original game, this facility is stationary and must be protected from seismic activity.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Restore the Research Core">
    Immediately craft a Repair Kit (10 Metal) to fix the Research Lab. Without this, you cannot start the "Forced Dormancy" questline.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Secure the Cryosleep Chamber">
    This is your 14-day hard deadline. It requires 50 Metal and 65 Rapidium. Ensure you have a clear soil cell in the base layout for its placement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage the Organics Buffer">
    Each person entering cryosleep requires 50 Organics. If you have Jan Scientist and two Alters, you must have 150 Organics stored before the cycle ends.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Final Day Checklist">
    Dismantle surface structures like Outposts or Extractors if the cycle warning indicates they will be destroyed. This returns 100% of the materials to your storage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="The 14-Day Trap">
Many players fail because they spend Metal on "Comfort" modules or "Dormitories" before securing the Cryosleep Chamber. Always build the Chamber first; Jan and the Alters can tolerate low morale for a few days, but they cannot survive the hot phase without cryosleep.
</Callout>

The expansion concludes with a major ethical dilemma regarding the Oasis and Jan Scientist's legacy. Your choices throughout the game—especially how you treat your Alters and which ones you "sacrifice" to the passage of time in Field Labs—will flavor the final moments.

### The Final Branch: Succession vs. Closure

| Choice | Theme | Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Create a Successor** | Obsession | Jan and JanBot continue the research through an endless cycle of new Alters. |
